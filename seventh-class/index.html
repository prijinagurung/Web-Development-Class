Etymology

The name "Sunday", the day of the Sun, is derived from the traditional astronomical naming system for days of the week. This system likely originated in the Neo-Babylonian Empire during the Jewish exile in Babylon, though the first direct evidence of it dates to the Roman Empire. The order has been explained in Hellenistic astrology as relating to planetary hours.
